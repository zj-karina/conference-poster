Sparse Features Mediate Refusal in Chat Models

A. Researcher, B. Coauthor, C. Advisor
MATS · University of Example · Example AI Safety Lab
A single sparse feature controls whether the model refuses — ablate it and refusals drop 88%. We localize refusal to one interpretable direction and steer it causally, with no retraining.
Scan for the paper
Figure 1. Ablating feature #4711 collapses the refusal direction across 5 model families, while held-out capabilities stay flat.

Problem

Safety behaviors in chat models are opaque: we don't know where refusal lives or whether it's a single mechanism. That makes it hard to audit or steer.

Method

  • Train a sparse autoencoder on residual-stream activations.
  • Rank features by causal effect on refusal logits.
  • Validate by ablation + activation steering on held-out prompts.
